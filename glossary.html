Glossary

Mariculture

The cultivation of fish or other marine life (i.e. clams) for food.

Social Complexity

    • Refers to the degree to which political roles, institution and processes are centralized and differentiated from other aspects of social organization.
      An overarching theme in Northwest Coast anthropology that frames culture and society as evolving in a unilinear fashion from ‘simple’ to ‘complex’ in all aspects of their organization.

    • Hunter Gatherers

    An economic categorization used by anthropologists to describe those who are primarily nomadic, have egalitarian social relations and live predominantly by hunting, fishing and harvesting wild food. Northwest Coast peoples are anomalously characterized as ‘complex’ hunter gatherers due to the fact that they lived in large sedentary villages with ranked social inequality, despite their reliance on hunting, fishing and harvesting strategies.

    Geomorphology

    The study of the physical features of the surface of the earth and their relation to its geological structures.

    Shell Midden

    A mound or deposit containing shells, animal bones, and other refuse indicating human occupation or settlement. From Danish, midden translates to ‘material that accumulates around a dwelling place’. In some cases, midden was used as an architectural material.

    Shell Hash

    Recycled shell material used to create and maintain clam garden terraces. Much like fertilizer, returning crushed up shell to the sediment increases the quantity of carbonate, which is attractive to clam larvae when they are looking for somewhere to settle.

    Barnacle Scar

    The remains from the base of a barnacle. They can be preserved on buried rocks within a clam garden wall and as organic material, they can be radiocarbon dated.

    OSL Dating

    A dating method used by archaeologists to determine when a material, such as sand, was last exposed to light (i.e. when it was buried). The luminescence of a material is determined by measuring the amount of electrons within it. Much like film, this dating method is extremely light sensitive, and any exposure to light during the process will damage the sample. Unlike radiocarbon dating, organic material is not required.

    Radiocarbon dating

    A dating method used to date organic materials. It measures the amount of carbon 14 within a sample, which has a known half-life and decays at a stable rate over time.

    Intertidal

    Literally meaning “between tides”. The intertidal zone is the middle section of a beach that is at times submerged at high tide, and exposed at low tide. The intertidal zone is highly productive environment that is rich in biodiversity.

    Topography

    The arrangement of natural and artificial physical features on a surface area
